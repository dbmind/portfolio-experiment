As the Senior Visual Designer volunteer at Apart of Me, I worked closely with the UX team, CEO, and production crew to support a mission-led platform helping young people through grief. I pitched creative ideas across brand, digital, and campaign design — always balancing sensitivity with clarity. In a space where emotion meets interaction, design had to be human, warm, and honest.

**Role:** Volunteer Senior Visual Designer — Branding design · London, UK

Apart of Me is a charity dedicated to supporting children, young people, and parents through loss and trauma.

![Apart of Me logo — the existing brand mark we worked from](/images/projects/apart-of-me/logo.png)

**Problem:** The absence of consistent branding across projects was diluting the organisation's message and impact. Without a unified visual identity, communications felt fragmented — which ultimately affected engagement and reduced donation potential.

**Plan:** Work with the existing logo while building out a clear, flexible set of branding guidelines. The goal: refine visual messaging across all touchpoints, strengthen brand recognition, and support the charity's mission with clarity and consistency.

![Mood board — setting the tone and visual direction](/images/projects/apart-of-me/000.png)

↑ We started with a mood board to set the tone and align on the right visual direction — gathering references that captured the emotion, warmth, and clarity the brand needed.

![Typography system — accessible, multilingual, considered](/images/projects/apart-of-me/100.png)

Typography had to be chosen with care — balancing accessibility, multilingual support, and cost. Especially during a time of global uncertainty, it was essential that our message could be communicated clearly across multiple languages and platforms, without barriers.

The colour palette was designed to carry meaning, not just style. We named pink "Compassion", yellow "Light", and blue "Healing" — anchoring each tone to the emotional values behind the charity's mission.

Accessibility was equally important. We ensured strong contrast and clarity across all combinations, creating a system that felt warm, readable, and inclusive — even when dealing with sensitive content. It was about designing with empathy at every level.
