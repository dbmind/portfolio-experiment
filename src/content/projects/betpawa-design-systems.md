At betPawa, I built and maintained an evolving set of component libraries and brand foundations in Figma — not just to tidy things up, but to make design move faster, cleaner, and smarter across countries and teams. These weren't just sticker sheets — they were living systems that empowered designers to stay on-brand while experimenting with freedom. Tokens, text styles, grids, responsive components, and brand elements were all baked into libraries that felt like tools, not rules.

I made it easier for Junior Designers to onboard and for Seniors to focus on solving problems — not reinventing buttons. By keeping our design DNA centralised and up-to-date, we could adapt fast to local needs without breaking the system.

**Role:** Branding Design · Digital UI/UX · AI Imagery · AI Integration · Social media campaigns

To speed up review cycles and ensure messaging consistency, we built a side-by-side localisation layout in Figma — grouping performance banners by language and format in a single shared frame.

This setup made it easy to spot translation issues, align tone, and roll out last-minute changes across 6+ languages with confidence. It also allowed the team to visually compare layouts and maintain creative balance across markets at a glance.

![Localisation workspace — banners side by side across 6+ languages](/images/projects/betpawa-design-systems/600.jpg)

We designed a localisation workspace in Figma that allowed the team to review, compare, and edit banners across multiple languages side-by-side — from English and French to Swahili, Wolof, and beyond. This setup drastically reduced back-and-forth with translation teams and made cross-market QA faster and more visual. With everything laid out in a single scroll, we could spot alignment issues, check consistency, and push updates quickly across entire campaigns.

## Onboard, inspire and nurture.

Whether a new Designer was about to onboard or needed a creative reset, I was often their first point of contact when it came to visuals and Figma systems. We'd start by exploring what made the brand tick — from texture and composition to design rhythm — before diving into the Figma ecosystem that powered our day-to-day. It was about building not just skill, but shared visual instinct.

Working in a remote-first setup meant being intentional with visibility and support. I maintained a strong presence across teams by staying responsive, joining cross-functional reviews, and often recording quick walkthroughs to guide others through Figma libraries, file hygiene, or design decisions. Whether async or live, I made sure Designers — especially juniors — felt supported, aligned, and empowered to do their best work.

Branded presentation layouts that echoed the visual DNA of betPawa were also in place — bold typography, high-contrast colour, and regional identity baked into the frame. Without overcomplicating things, the decks remained clean, consistent, and unmistakably on-brand, helping teams across 20+ markets communicate ideas with clarity and visual unity.

For our social media, we leaned into country-specific flavour, tone, and cultural relevance. From local holidays to national sports wins and everyday expressions, each post was crafted to feel native — not generic. In a continent as diverse as Africa, this approach helped audiences see themselves in the brand, building trust and relatability across regions.
