I introduced the AI movement to our design team — not as a gimmick, but as a creative extension of our toolkit. From day one, I explored, tested, and shared workflows that helped us move faster and think wider. I also trained Designers on how to prompt, tweak, and integrate AI-generated visuals into real brand work with purpose and control.

In a space where many products rely on the same stock sources, I pushed to give betPawa a distinct, ownable visual feel — especially across photography, character design, and brand imagery. AI became less of a shortcut and more of a creative partner — helping us move fast without losing originality.

Using Midjourney intensively for campaign visuals, ChatGPT to generate structured image prompts, and Adobe Firefly for asset detailing and 3D compositions, we built a unique brand world that stood apart from the competition.

Creating unique characters took countless rounds of trial and error. There were always misfires, tweaks, and last-minute amends — even after a "final" render. But we embraced that as part of the process. It was a creative and collaborative rhythm, where each iteration brought us closer to characters that felt right for the brand, the region, and the story we wanted to tell.

![Midjourney SREFs and style development](/images/projects/betpawa-ai/200.png)

Having worked with Midjourney since its early days, I developed a strong eye for crafting custom SREFs, image styles, and visual prompts that gave our campaigns a unique edge. This expertise didn't just support production — it also helped spark inspiration, unlock visual directions, and push our creative boundaries beyond the usual stock-driven look.

From there, we'd collaborate closely as a team, shaping and refining characters or assets until they evolved into fully-formed key visuals. It was a mix of creative instinct, technical finesse, and iteration — turning fast ideas into campaign-defining imagery.
