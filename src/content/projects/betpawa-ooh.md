During my time at betPawa, most campaigns were developed in-house — giving us full control to connect the dots between product, performance, and presence. From digital banners to event activations and OOH, we kept the brand identity consistent while adapting to the scale and energy of each space. Out-of-home was always a chance to go bolder — stretching the typography, amplifying colour, and letting the visual language breathe at 1:1 scale.

With betPawa active in over 15 countries, it was key to ensure every OOH piece landed right — visually, culturally, and strategically. I worked closely with marketing coordinators, the CMO, and regional stakeholders to stay on top of local requirements, align messaging, and deliver assets that felt native but brand-consistent. Whether it was a pitch-side banner in Kenya or a booth in Malawi, every execution was part of a bigger, connected identity.
